Chirp Alarm — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Chirp Alarm is a paid iOS app with no ads and no subscription. This policy describes what data the app collects, where it goes, and what it does not do.

Data stored on your device

Chirp Alarm stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Your alarm settings (time, volume, enabled state)
  • Your selected bird species and downloaded recordings
  • Your quiz history and statistics
  • Your language preference

This data is saved in a local file on your device. It is never transmitted to any server and is not accessible to the developer.

Network requests

When you search for bird species or download recordings, the app makes requests to xeno-canto.org, a third-party community library of bird field recordings. These requests include your search terms and the recording IDs you select. Please refer to the xeno-canto terms of use for their data practices.

No other network requests are made. There is no analytics service, no crash reporting service, and no advertising network in this app.

Data the developer cannot access

The developer has no backend server and receives no data from your use of the app. Your alarms, species selections, and quiz results exist only on your device.

Third-party SDKs

Chirp Alarm contains no third-party SDKs or tracking libraries. It uses only Apple's system frameworks.

Children

Chirp Alarm does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices change in a future version, this page will be updated. Significant changes will be noted in the App Store release notes.
